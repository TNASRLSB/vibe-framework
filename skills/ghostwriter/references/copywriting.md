## Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

The classic direct response framework. Works for any content that needs to persuade.

**Attention:** Grab attention with a bold headline, shocking stat, or provocative question.
**Interest:** Build interest by showing you understand their situation. Present relevant information.
**Desire:** Create desire by painting the outcome — what life looks like after the solution.
**Action:** Drive action with a clear, specific CTA.

**Example — SaaS Landing Page:**
> **A:** Your team wastes 11 hours per week in meetings that should have been emails.
> **I:** Meeting overload costs companies $25,000 per employee annually in lost productivity. The average knowledge worker attends 62 meetings per month — and considers more than half of them unnecessary.
> **D:** Imagine reclaiming 40% of your team's meeting time. AsyncWork replaces status meetings with structured async updates, giving your team focused work blocks back.
> **A:** Start your free 14-day trial — no credit card required.

**Best for:** Landing pages, email sequences, product launches, sales letters.

---

### PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution

The most versatile framework. Name the problem, make it hurt, then solve it.

**Problem:** State the problem clearly and specifically.
**Agitation:** Amplify the pain. What happens if they don't solve it? What are they losing?
**Solution:** Present your solution as the relief.

**Example — Email Subject + Body:**
> **P:** Your blog posts aren't ranking — and you don't know why.
> **A:** You've published 50 articles. Spent months writing. Promoted on social media. But Google sends you 200 visitors a month. Meanwhile, competitors with worse content rank above you. Every month you don't fix this, they pull further ahead — and your content investment depreciates.
> **S:** Our SEO audit identifies the exact technical and content issues killing your rankings. In 72 hours, you get a prioritized fix list. Most clients see 40-80% traffic improvement within 90 days.

**Best for:** Email marketing, problem-aware audiences, B2B sales, blog intros.

---

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Show the transformation. What is life like now vs. what it could be?

**Before:** Describe the current painful reality.
**After:** Paint the desired future state.
**Bridge:** Your product/service is the bridge between them.

**Example — Product Description:**
> **B:** Right now, your design team juggles 6 different tools for wireframing, prototyping, design, handoff, version control, and feedback. Context-switching costs 23 minutes per interruption.
> **A:** One tool. Wireframe → design → prototype → handoff → feedback, all in one workspace. Your team ships 3x faster because they never leave the flow.
> **B:** Designflow unifies your entire design workflow. Import your existing Figma files today.

**Best for:** Product pages, case studies, testimonials, social media ads.

### 4 Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

A structured persuasion sequence.

**Promise:** Make a specific, believable promise.
**Picture:** Help them visualize having the result.
**Proof:** Back it up with evidence.
**Push:** Nudge toward action.

**Example — Course Sales Page:**
> **Promise:** Learn to write landing pages that convert at 5%+ — in 6 weeks.
> **Picture:** Imagine launching a page and watching signups roll in. No more guessing. No more "let's try a different headline." You'll know exactly why each element works.
> **Proof:** 2,400 graduates. Average conversion rate improvement: 340%. Featured in ConversionXL, CopyHackers, and MarketingProfs.
> **Push:** Enrollment closes Friday. 30-day money-back guarantee. Start Module 1 today.

**Best for:** Courses, premium products, high-ticket services.

---

### FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits

Transform feature lists into persuasive copy.

**Feature:** What it is (technical fact).
**Advantage:** What it does (functional benefit).
**Benefit:** Why it matters (emotional/business outcome).

**Example:**
> **Feature:** 256-bit AES encryption
> **Advantage:** Your data is encrypted with military-grade security at rest and in transit
> **Benefit:** Sleep at night knowing a breach won't expose customer data or trigger compliance violations

**Rule:** Always lead with benefits in headlines and opening copy. Features go in spec sections.

**Best for:** Product descriptions, feature pages, technical products needing translation for non-technical buyers.

---

### PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Transformation, Offer, Response

Extended framework for long-form sales pages.

**Problem:** Identify the specific pain.
**Amplify:** Make it urgent — what happens if they don't act?
**Story:** Tell a relevant story (founder story, customer story, analogy).
**Transformation:** Show the before/after clearly.
**Offer:** Present what they get (stack the value).
**Response:** Tell them exactly what to do next.

**Best for:** Long-form sales pages, webinar scripts, video sales letters.

## Headline Formulas

Headlines determine whether anyone reads the rest. These formulas are proven performers.

### Number Headlines
```
[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]
[Number] [Keyword] Mistakes That [Negative Consequence]
[Number] [Keyword] Tips [Audience] Wish They Knew Sooner
[Number] [Keyword] Strategies That [Specific Result] in [Timeframe]
[Number] Reasons [Common Belief] Is Wrong
```

**Examples:**
- 7 Landing Page Mistakes That Kill Your Conversion Rate
- 15 Email Subject Lines That Get 40%+ Open Rates
- 21 SEO Techniques Enterprise Sites Use to Dominate Rankings

### How-To Headlines
```
How to [Achieve Result] (Without [Common Objection])
How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe]
How to [Achieve Result] Even If [Obstacle]
How [Specific Person/Company] [Achieved Result]
How to Go from [Bad State] to [Good State] in [Timeframe]
```

**Examples:**
- How to Write Headlines That Get Clicks (Without Clickbait)
- How to Double Your Email List in 90 Days
- How to Rank on Page 1 Even If You Have Zero Backlinks
- How Stripe Built a $95B Company on Developer Experience

### Question Headlines
```
What If You Could [Desired Outcome]?
Are You Making These [Number] [Keyword] Mistakes?
Why Do [Group] [Unexpected Behavior]?
What [Respected Source] Knows About [Topic] That You Don't
Is [Common Practice] Actually [Negative Outcome]?
```

### Power Statement Headlines
```
The [Keyword] [Framework/System/Method] That [Result]
The Only [Keyword] Guide You'll Ever Need
Everything You Know About [Topic] Is Wrong
Stop [Common Mistake]. Start [Better Alternative].
[Bold Claim]: Why [Counterintuitive Statement]
```

### Specificity Headlines
```
We [Action] and [Specific Result Happened]
I [Action] for [Timeframe] — Here's What [Happened]
[Specific Number] [Result] in [Specific Timeframe]: The Exact [Method]
From [Specific Bad State] to [Specific Good State] in [Timeframe]
```

**Examples:**
- We Tested 1,000 Subject Lines — These 7 Patterns Won Every Time
- I Wrote 100 Blog Posts in 2025 — Here's What Actually Drove Traffic
- From 200 to 50,000 Monthly Visitors in 6 Months: The Exact Content Strategy

## CTA Patterns

A CTA is the moment of conversion. Every word matters.

### CTA Button Text Formulas

**Benefit-first (highest performing):**
```
Get [Benefit] →
Start [Doing Desired Thing] →
Claim Your [Offer] →
See [Result] in Action →
```

**Action-oriented:**
```
Start Free Trial
Download the Guide
Get Instant Access
Join [Number]+ [Peers]
Create My Account
Book a Demo
```

**Urgency (use ethically — only when real):**
```
Claim Your Spot (Only [X] Left)
Start Before [Deadline]
Get [Offer] — Ends [Date]
```

### CTA Surrounding Copy

The text around a CTA button matters as much as the button itself.

**Above the CTA:**
- Restate the value proposition in one sentence
- Or include a testimonial snippet

**Below the CTA:**
- Reduce friction: "No credit card required" / "Cancel anytime" / "Takes 30 seconds"
- Social proof: "Join 10,000+ marketers" / "Rated 4.9/5 by 500+ customers"
- Risk reversal: "30-day money-back guarantee"

### CTA Placement

- **First CTA:** Above the fold (for ready-to-act visitors)
- **Second CTA:** After the value demonstration section
- **Final CTA:** At page bottom, after all proof and objection handling
- **Sticky CTA:** Consider a sticky header or footer bar for long pages
- **Max:** 3 CTA instances per page (same action, can vary wording)

## Persuasion Psychology

These principles are backed by decades of behavioral research. Use them ethically.

### Scarcity

People value things more when they are limited.

**Legitimate applications:**
- Limited-time offers (with real deadlines)
- Limited quantity ("Only 12 spots remaining" — when true)
- Exclusive access ("Beta access for first 500 signups")

**Never fabricate scarcity.** Fake countdown timers and artificial limits destroy trust permanently.

### Social Proof

People follow what others do, especially similar others.

**Types (ranked by strength):**
1. **Specific testimonials** with name, photo, role, company, and specific result
2. **Case studies** with before/after metrics
3. **Numbers:** "10,000+ customers" / "2M downloads"
4. **Logos:** Client or press logos
5. **Ratings:** "4.8/5 on G2" with star visualization
6. **User-generated content:** Real reviews, social posts, community activity

**Rule:** Generic testimonials ("Great product!") are worthless. Specific testimonials ("Increased our conversion rate from 1.2% to 4.7% in 3 months") are gold.

### Authority

People defer to experts and credible sources.

**How to signal authority:**
- Author bylines with credentials
- "Featured in [Publication]" badges
- Certifications and partnerships
- Data and research citations
- Industry awards
- Expert endorsements

### Reciprocity

When you give value first, people feel compelled to reciprocate.

**Applications:**
- Free tools, templates, calculators
- Valuable free content (not gated behind email)
- Free trial with full functionality
- Free consultation or audit
- Educational email sequences

### Commitment and Consistency

Once people take a small action, they are more likely to take a larger one.

**Applications:**
- Start with a micro-commitment: "Take our free quiz" → leads to product recommendation
- Multi-step forms (complete step 1 → motivated to finish)
- Free trial → paid conversion
- Newsletter → webinar → purchase

### Loss Aversion

People are more motivated by avoiding loss than gaining equivalent value.

**Applications:**
- "Don't miss out on..."
- "Stop losing [X] every month"
- "See what you're leaving on the table"
- Free trial that adds value → losing it feels costly

### Anchoring

The first number people see frames all subsequent numbers.

**Applications:**
- Show the "full price" before the discounted price
- Compare to the cost of the problem: "This bug costs you $5,000/month. Fix it for $199."
- Enterprise tier listed first to make standard tier feel affordable

## Writing Mechanics for Persuasion

### Sentence Length for Impact

- **Short sentences punch.** Use them for key claims.
- Medium sentences carry the explanation and reasoning behind those claims.
- Long sentences work when you need to connect multiple ideas, show relationships, or build a rhythm that carries the reader from one thought to the next before resolving with a strong conclusion.
- **Pattern:** Long → short. Build up → deliver.

### Paragraph Structure

- One idea per paragraph
- Lead with the most important sentence
- 2-4 sentences maximum for web content
- Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis (use sparingly — max 2 per page)

### Voice and Tone

**Active voice** (default): "We designed this tool to save you time."
**Passive voice** (rare, intentional): "This report was reviewed by three independent analysts." (when the actor matters less than the action)

- Address the reader directly: "you" and "your"
- Write as if speaking to one person, not an audience
- Match the audience's vocabulary level
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Be specific — vague copy is weak copy

### The "So What?" Test

After every sentence, paragraph, and section, ask: "So what? Why should the reader care?"

If the answer is not obvious, either:
1. Add the "so what" — connect the statement to a reader benefit
2. Cut it — if it doesn't serve the reader, it doesn't belong

## Content Type Copywriting Quick Reference

### Homepage

- Hero headline: benefit-driven, under 10 words
- Subheadline: elaborate on the promise, 15-25 words
- 3 value propositions visible without scrolling
- Social proof near the top
- One primary CTA

### About Page

- Lead with the customer's problem, not your history
- Story framework: "We saw [problem] → We built [solution] → Now [result]"
- Team credibility without ego
- Values that connect to customer benefits

### Pricing Page

- Anchor with highest tier first (or highlight recommended tier)
- Feature comparison table
- FAQ addressing common objections
- Money-back guarantee prominently displayed
- Annual vs monthly with savings highlighted

### Email Subject Lines

**Formulas:**
```
[Number] [keyword] [modifier] (e.g., "5 SEO mistakes killing your traffic")
How to [result] in [timeframe] (e.g., "How to double leads in 30 days")
[Name], [personalized question] (e.g., "Sarah, still struggling with churn?")
Don't [action] until you [read/see] this
Quick question about [their specific situation]
[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out
```

**Rules:**
- Under 50 characters for mobile visibility
- No ALL CAPS
- No excessive punctuation (!!!)
- Avoid spam triggers: "free," "act now," "limited time" in subject line
- Personalization increases open rates by 26% on average
- Preview text (preheader) is your second headline — don't waste it

### Microcopy (UI Text)

- Error messages: say what went wrong AND how to fix it
- Empty states: guide the next action ("No projects yet. Create your first one.")
- Loading states: set expectations ("Analyzing your data — this takes about 10 seconds")
- Success states: confirm and suggest next step ("Payment confirmed! Check your email for receipt.")
- Button labels: verb + noun ("Download Report" not "Submit")
- Form labels: clear, concise, no jargon
- Tooltips: one sentence, answers "what is this?"
