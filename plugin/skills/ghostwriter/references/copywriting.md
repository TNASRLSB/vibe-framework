# Copywriting Process Constraints

This reference defines **mandatory process constraints** for producing copy. It does not teach copywriting frameworks or techniques — you already know them. It enforces discipline that prevents generic, AI-sounding output.

---

## 1. Audience Modeling (Mandatory — Before Any Writing)

Before writing a single word of copy, complete all four:

1. **Reader's perspective:** Write 3 sentences as the reader — what they think, what they feel, what they fear. Not demographics. Their inner dialogue.
2. **Belief shift:** What does the reader believe now that you need to change? One sentence.
3. **Scroll stopper:** What specific thing would make this person stop scrolling? Not "a bold headline" — what SPECIFIC claim, image, or question?
4. **Single message:** If the reader remembers ONE thing, what must it be? One sentence max.

Do not proceed to writing without completing these. They are the foundation.

---

## 2. Multiple Options (Mandatory — During Writing)

Never ship the first acceptable output. Generate options, then select:

| Element | Options to generate | Select |
|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Headline | 5 | 1 best |
| Opening hook | 3 | 1 best |
| CTA text | 3 | 1 best |
| Value proposition framing | 3 | 1 best |

For each selection, state WHY this option is strongest. "It's the best" is not a reason.

---

## 3. Anti-AI Pattern Detection (Mandatory — After First Draft)

These patterns signal AI-generated copy. Detect and eliminate every instance:

**Structural patterns:**
- Excessive parallel tricolon ("faster, smarter, better" / "save time, save money, save effort")
- Lists where every item has identical grammatical structure AND length
- Perfect symmetry in consecutive sentences or paragraphs
- Every section following the same internal structure (statement → elaboration → example)
- Three-adjective chains before nouns ("our innovative, cutting-edge, revolutionary platform")

**Opening patterns:**
- Gerund phrase openers ("Leveraging the power of...", "Transforming how you...")
- "Imagine a world where..." / "Picture this:" / "What if you could..."
- Starting with a rhetorical question that nobody actually asks

**Word-level patterns:**
- Generic intensifiers replacing specifics ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "robust")
- Abstract benefits without proof ("boost productivity," "streamline workflows," "drive growth")
- Adverb hedging ("significantly," "dramatically," "seamlessly," "effortlessly")
- Corporate buzzwords as filler ("synergy," "ecosystem," "holistic," "paradigm")

**Rhythm patterns:**
- Every paragraph the same length
- Monotonous sentence rhythm (medium-medium-medium instead of short-long-short)
- Conclusions that mechanically summarize everything just said

**The test:** Read the draft pretending you are a cynical reader. If at any point you think "this sounds like AI wrote it" — that section needs rewriting.

---

## 4. Sharpening (Mandatory — After Anti-AI Check)

Five passes, in order:

1. **Cut 20%** of total words. If it doesn't hurt to lose a sentence, it wasn't earning its place.
2. **Abstract → Concrete:** Every claim like "improve efficiency" becomes specific: "cut report generation from 4 hours to 20 minutes."
3. **Generic → Specific:** Every sentence that could appear unchanged on a competitor's site gets rewritten with the user's unique angle.
4. **Read aloud test:** Does it sound like a person talking, or a template filled in? Rewrite anything that sounds templated.
5. **Quotability test:** Is there ONE sentence a reader would share with a colleague? If not, the piece lacks a memorable core — find it and sharpen it.

---

## 5. The "So What?" Gate

After every sentence, paragraph, and section: "So what? Why should the reader care?"

If the answer isn't obvious:
- Add the "so what" — connect the statement to a reader benefit
- Or cut it — if it doesn't serve the reader, it doesn't belong

---

## 6. CTA Placement

- **First CTA:** Above the fold (for ready-to-act visitors)
- **Second CTA:** After value demonstration section
- **Final CTA:** At page bottom, after proof and objection handling
- **Max:** 3 CTA instances per page (same action, can vary wording)
- **Above CTA:** Restate value proposition in one sentence, or include a testimonial
- **Below CTA:** Friction reducer ("No credit card required" / "Cancel anytime" / "30-day guarantee")

---

## 7. Microcopy

- **Error messages:** What went wrong + how to fix it
- **Empty states:** Guide the next action ("No projects yet. Create your first one.")
- **Loading states:** Set expectations ("Analyzing your data — about 10 seconds")
- **Success states:** Confirm + suggest next step ("Payment confirmed! Check your email.")
- **Button labels:** Verb + noun ("Download Report" not "Submit")
- **Form labels:** Clear, no jargon
- **Tooltips:** One sentence, answers "what is this?"
